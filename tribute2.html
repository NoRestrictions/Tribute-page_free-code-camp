Dr. Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala

A woman of rare substance



Here is a time line of Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala's life:

  • 1954 - Born in Ogwashi-Ukwu, Delta State, Nigeria.
  • 1976 - Graduates with an AB in Economics.
  • 1981 - Earns her PhD in Regional Economics and Development.
  • 2003 - Becomes Nigeria's Minister of Finance, in which she leads efforts to improve Nigeria's macro-economic management.
  • 2007 - Becomes World Bank's Managing Director.
  • 2008 - Spearheads several World Bank initiatives to assist low-income countries in the 2008-2009 food crises.
  • 2010 - Chair of the IDA replenishment, World Bank’s successful drive to raise $49.3 billion in grants and low interest credit for the poorest countries in the world.
  • 2011 - Becomes Minister of Finance in Nigeria for a second tenure, with the expanded portfolio of the Coordinating Minister for the Economy by President Goodluck Jonathan. Her legacy includes strengthening Nigeria’s public financial systems, stimulating the housing sector with the establishment of the Nigerian Mortgage Refinance Corporation (NMRC).[14] She also empowered Nigeria’s women and youth with the Growing Girls and Women in Nigeria Programme (GWIN); a gender responsive budgeting system[15] and the highly acclaimed Youth Enterprise with Innovation programme (YouWIN); a highly acclaimed programme to support entrepreneurs that created thousands of jobs.[16]
  • 2015 - Joins Lazard as a Senior Advisor.
  • 2016 - Becomes Chair of the Global Alliance for Vaccines and Immunization (GAVI).
